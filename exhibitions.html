#portraitsfornhsheroes

This online portrait exhibition of NHS Key Workers is an intiative created by @TomCroftArtist on Instagram. During the unprecedented times of Covid-19 pandemic of the year 2020, the NHS Key Workers are our frontline health heroes and I was inspired to take part in this project whilst in lockdown. I am honoured to have the privilege to paint an NHS health hero and it has shone a positive light in these challenging times, a way to show my gesture of appreciation and thanks.

Fusion

(SOLD)

2018

140cm x 100cm

Giclée laminate matte print on Foamex board

This self-portrait sets two distinctive culinary styles against each other, exploring the conflictions of cultural duality. Baked beans in a Chinese bowl, English Breakfast in a Chinese teapot, a fried egg eaten over rice with chopsticks.

Permanent artwork for Student Art Collection at Oxford Brookes University

Installation at the Hoi Polloi Exhibition 2018 at Oxford Brookes University

Where Do You Really Come From?

2018

30cm x 50cm

Digital paintings, 1 min loop videos

A series of digitally painted self-portraits that takes a playful approach to tackling the issue of ethnic stereotyping, editing my own facial features to assimilate my identity into the very stereotypes others impose upon myself through their assumptions of my ethnicity. Chinese, Tibetan, Korean and Japanese.

Video installations at the Hoi Polloi Exhibition 2018 at Oxford Brookes University

Merging each identity in a fluid, mirrored and off-sync manner.

Adobe Photoshop, Adobe After Effects
